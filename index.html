UVAS Student Accounting Society Elections

The Utah Valley Accounting Society (UVAS) is the student accounting society at UVU. We help students get involved in the department, stay engaged with the profession, and build meaningful connections with firms and industry employers.

UVAS supports firm visits to campus, networking opportunities, hiring and resume recruitment conversations, and regular student-facing activities. We plan events, coordinate classroom visits, and stay actively connected with organizations such as the Utah Association of Certified Public Accountants (UACPA), the Utah chapter of the AICPA.

Why Students Join UVAS

  • Department involvement and leadership experience
  • Direct networking with firms and industry employers
  • Exposure to hiring and resume recruitment conversations
  • Professional engagement with UACPA and the AICPA

About the Election Process

Students can review officer positions below, submit candidacy information during the nomination window, and vote once nominations close. Election results will be announced at the end-of-semester event.

Appointed roles are selected separately from elections: President is appointed by faculty advisors, and UACPA Ambassadors (2) are appointed.

Any member of the Accounting Society is welcome to run for a position, but please know that serving comes with a significant time commitment. You will be expected to help for at least one semester while you are an elected officer.

This is strong resume experience that you can show to employers and other professional contacts. The networking and firm interactions, as well as the leadership training, are the best part of being a member.

Elected Officer Positions

Available elected positions are listed below. “Relations” officers are responsible for Communications.

  • Events Officer
  • Activities Officer
  • Firm Relations Officer
  • CAS and Industry Relations Officer
  • Student and Faculty Relations Officer
  • Competitions Officer
  • Historian

Events Officer – Responsibilities include:

  • With assistance from faculty advisors, recruit and lead a team of two to three others
  • Plan Meet the Firms each semester and other large events, such as kickoff meetings, panel presentations, Etiquette Luncheon/Dinner, Career Day, etc.
  • This is a big and fun responsibility and may include setting up logistical details, getting quotes from different vendors, working within a budget, managing RSVP’s and other similar event planning details
  • Provide info to relations officers to get the word out

Activities Officer – Responsibilities include:

  • With assistance from faculty advisors, recruit and lead a team of one or two others
  • Scheduling and managing logistics for bi-weekly presentations, open houses, and class visits

Firm Relations Officer – Responsibilities include:

  • Working with President, Events Officer, and Activities Officer to communicate with Firms about upcoming involvement and events
  • Manage responses from firms and follow-up communications

CAS and Industry Relations Officer – Responsibilities include:

  • Develop and maintain processes to connect industry organizations with our students
  • Work with David Waite and his group to aid in this development
  • Working with President, Events Officer, and Activities Officer to communicate with industry and government organizations about upcoming involvement and events
  • Manage responses from with industry and government organizations and follow-up communications

Student and Faculty Relations Officer – Responsibilities include:

  • With assistance from faculty advisors, recruit and lead a team of one or two others
  • Send information to students about upcoming events and activities
  • Send information to faculty to distribute to students
  • Update info for display screens and elevators
  • Maintain a public calendar and website with UVAS information

Competitions Officer – Responsibilities include:

  • With assistance from faculty advisors, recruit and lead a team of one or two others
  • Organize case study and other competitions, including inviting other universities

Historian – Responsibilities include:

  • Take meeting minutes at BAP officer meetings and save for record keeping
  • Keep record of attendance at BAP events and presentations
  • Provide updates to Student and Faculty Relations Officer to update the website
  • Maintain a historical record of events and activities
